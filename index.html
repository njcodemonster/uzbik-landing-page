Follow the Path of Timur & the Scholars

Uzbekistan sits at the heart of the ancient Silk Road—a land where centuries of Islamic scholarship, art and architecture converge. From the turquoise domes of Samarkand to the labyrinthine alleys of Khiva's walled city, this itinerary traces the most profound Islamic heritage sites in Central Asia.

✈ Tashkent 🕌 Samarkand 🏛 Bukhara 🏰 Khiva
Day 01
Arrival in Tashkent
Capital City Welcome

Arrive at Tashkent International Airport and transfer to your hotel. After settling in, begin exploring Tashkent's rich Islamic quarter with an afternoon visit to the Khast-Imam Complex and the iconic Chorsu Bazaar. Evening stroll around Amir Timur Square.

  • Khast-Imam Complex & Barak-Khan Madrasah
  • Mausoleum of Kaffal Shashi (Sheikh Hovendi At-Takhur)
  • Islamic Civilization Center
  • Chorsu Bazaar (covered market, 16th c.)
  • Old City (Eski Shahar) walk
  • Amir Timur Square & Monument
✈ International Arrival · Hotel Check-in
✦ Day 2 ✦
Day 02
Tashkent in Depth
Museums, Metro & Islamic Arts

A full day discovering Tashkent's culture and history. Explore the magnificent Soviet-era Tashkent Metro stations, visit the State Museum of Applied Arts with its stunning Islamic tile and woodwork collections, and see the famous Courage Monument. Evening visit to the Alisher Navoi Opera Theatre.

  • State Museum of Applied Arts
  • Tashkent Metro (ornate stations)
  • Alisher Navoi Opera & Ballet Theatre
  • Courage Monument
  • Local Pottery & Ceramics Gallery (Rishtan)
  • Independence Square
🚇 Metro · 🚌 City Tour
✦ Day 3 ✦
Day 03
Tashkent → Samarkand
City of Timur · The Blue Domes

Board the high-speed Afrosiyob train to Samarkand (2 hrs). Arrive and immediately feel the grandeur of Timur's imperial city. Afternoon tour of the magnificent Registan Square—three towering madrasahs forming one of the world's most beautiful Islamic ensembles. Visit the Bibi-Khanum Mosque and the legendary Gur-Emir Mausoleum.

  • Registan Square (Ulugbek, Sher-Dor & Tilla-Kari Madrasahs)
  • Bibi-Khanum Mosque (15th c. Timurid masterpiece)
  • Siab Bazaar (city market)
  • Gur-Emir Mausoleum (Tomb of Timur)
  • Afrosiyob High-Speed Train Journey
🚄 Afrosiyob Train · 2 hrs · Hotel Check-in
✦ Day 4 ✦
Day 04
Samarkand in Depth
Necropolis, Observatory & Sacred Tombs

Begin the day at the breathtaking Shah-i-Zinda Necropolis, a 1 km alley of royal mausoleums clad in dazzling turquoise and cobalt tiles. Visit Ulugbek's 15th-century astronomical observatory, the Afrasiab Museum with its ancient pre-Islamic murals, and the Tomb of the Prophet Daniel (Hazrat Doniyor).

  • Shah-i-Zinda Necropolis (Avenue of Mausoleums)
  • Ulugbek Observatory (1428 AD)
  • Afrasiab Archaeological Museum
  • Tomb of Saint Daniel (Hazrat Doniyor)
  • Konigil Paper Mill (traditional handmade paper)
🚌 City Tour · Full Day in Samarkand
✦ Day 5 ✦
Day 05
Samarkand → Bukhara
The Holy City · UNESCO World Heritage

Drive to Bukhara (280 km, ~4 hrs), stopping en route at the Rabat-i-Malik Caravanserai ruins—a 11th-century Silk Road waystation. Arrive Bukhara and explore the Po-i-Kalyan ensemble: the soaring Kalyan Minaret, Miri-Arab Madrasah, and the peaceful Lyabi-Khauz plaza with its ancient mulberry trees.

  • Rabat-i-Malik Caravanserai (en-route stop)
  • Kalyan Minaret & Mosque (12th c.)
  • Miri-Arab Madrasah (16th c., still active)
  • Lyabi-Khauz Ensemble (old Jewish Quarter)
  • Ulugbek Madrasah & Abdulazizkhan Madrasah
  • Trading Domes (Taq bazaars)
🚗 Drive 280km · Roadside Stops · Hotel Check-in
✦ Day 6 ✦
Day 06
Bukhara in Depth
Ark Fortress, Shrines & Sacred Springs

A full day immersed in old Bukhara's sacred quarters. The mighty Ark Fortress dominates the city skyline as it has for 2,500 years. Visit the fairy-tale Chor-Minor Madrasah with its four minarets, the serene Samanid Mausoleum, and Chashma-Ayub (Spring of Job). Afternoon excursion to the Naqshbandi Shrine outside the city.

  • Ark Fortress (ancient royal citadel, 2500+ years old)
  • Bolo-Khauz Mosque (20-column mosque)
  • Chor-Minor Madrasah (Four Minarets)
  • Samanid Mausoleum (9th–10th c., earliest Islamic tomb)
  • Chashma-Ayub (Spring of Job mausoleum)
  • Mausoleum of Bakhouddin Naqshbandi
  • Sitorai Mokhi-Khosa (Summer Palace)
🚌 City Tour · Day Excursion
✦ Day 7 ✦
Day 07
Bukhara → Khiva
The Open-Air Museum · Itchan-Kala

Fly or drive to Urgench, then transfer to ancient Khiva—an extraordinarily preserved Silk Road city entirely enclosed within massive mud-brick walls. Walk through the west gate into Itchan-Kala, a UNESCO World Heritage site that feels unchanged since the 17th century. Explore the dazzling turquoise-tiled Kalta-Minor minaret and the forest of columns inside Juma Mosque.

  • Itchan-Kala Fortress (UNESCO World Heritage walled city)
  • Kalta-Minor Minaret (unfinished turquoise minaret)
  • Juma Mosque (212 carved wooden columns)
  • Mukhammad Aminkhan Madrasah
  • Mukhammad Rahimkhan Madrasah
  • Tash-Khovli Palace (harem complex)
✈ Flight to Urgench · 🚌 Transfer to Khiva
✦ Day 8 ✦
Day 08
Khiva & Departure
Final Exploration & Farewell

Spend the morning completing Khiva's highlights: climb the Islam-Khoja Minaret—the tallest in Khiva at 57 metres—for panoramic views over the ancient city. Pay respects at the beautifully tiled Pakhlavan Makhmud Mausoleum, the city's patron saint and national hero. Afternoon transfer to Urgench Airport for departure flight back to Tashkent or onward.

  • Islam-Khoja Minaret & Madrasah (tallest in Khiva, 1910)
  • Pakhlavan Makhmud Mausoleum (patron saint shrine)
  • Last walk through Itchan-Kala streets & souvenir bazaar
  • Transfer to Urgench Airport
  • Departure flight
🚌 Transfer to Urgench · ✈ Departure
